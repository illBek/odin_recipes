Dessert Options

  1. Tiramisu

    2. Description:

    Tiramisu is every person's favorite dessert (not yours? Stop lying). It is a layered cake, the structure of which is made with a special kind of biscuits (savoiardi), dipped in coffee (which gives it its name, literally, "pick me up"). A luxurious cream made by eggs, sugar and mascarpone is its binding element. Cacao powder should usually sprinkled on top, because why not?

      Ingredients:

    • 400g savoiardi
    • 6 eggs - at room temperature
    • 500g mascarpone
    • 100g sugar
    • cacao powder
    • coffee (approximately 2 cups)
    • a pinch of salt

      Process:

    1. Prepare the coffee - then let it cool at the bottom of a soup plate. You should be able to dip the savoiardi in it.
    2. Separate egg yolks from egg whites.
    3. Pour half of the total sugar into the bowl containing the egg yolks, then whisk until clear and bubbly.
    4. Add the mascarpone to the egg yolks + sugar mixture. Mix until well combined.
    5. Clean the whisk really well. After which, add a pinch of salt to the bowl containing the egg whites, add some of the remaining sugar and start whisking. Add some more sugar after a while, and finish it off after some more time.
    6. When the above is super firm (it shouldn't fall off the whisk when lifted), add some to the mascarpone mixture, mix it in, then add the remaining in separate tranches, mixing from top to bottom and incorporating it delicately.
    7. When the whole cream is homogeneous and absolutely delicious, you can start putting it all together. It shouldn't be liquid - soggy and liquid-y tiramisu are the worst!
    8. In an oven pan, spread a tablespoon of cream at the bottom. Then dip the savoiardi (half way, otherwise they become too soggy) and lay them flat covering the whole oven pan.
    9. Add a generous portion of the cream on top - nobody likes dry tiramisu either! Then sprinkle with cacao powder.
    10. Repeat until you run out of cream or savoiardi. With these quantities, you should be able to do 2/3 layers of a 35cm x 18cm oven pan (approximately).
    11. Let it rest, in the fridge, for at least 6 hours before eating (overnight, possibly. A full day if you really love food). Finish it within 3 days - it shouldn't be a problem, really.

  2. Ricotta, Amaretti and Chocolate cake - Gluten-free

    3. Description:

    This cake is an absolute killer. It's hard to describe, but it is easy to love and even easier to marry - until diabetes do us part.

      Ingredients:

    • 100g buckwheat flour
    • 100g rice flour
    • 60g coconut sugar
    • 30ml coconut oil
    • 2 tsp vanilla extract
    • 75ml vegetable milk (approx)

      Process:

    1. In a bowl, add all the solid ingredients, followed by the liquid ingredients.
    2. Once happy with the consistency (they should be runny but not liquid, able to keep the shape in the pan) warm up a pan and melt some coconut oil.
    3. Pour 1 fat tablespoon of batter onto the hot pan, cover and let it cook until bubbles appear - after which turn them and let them cook on the other side as well.
    4. Once enough of them are ready, eat with a sprinkle of soy yogurt, some seasonal fruit and some maple / coconut syrup.
